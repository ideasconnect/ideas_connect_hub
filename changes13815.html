Help Guide

Difference between Act and Regulations:
An Act is a legal document providing a general or broad description of legal policies.

Regulations are guidelines associated with an Act that guide how provisions of an Act are applied and should be followed.

Difference between Legislation and Regulation:
Legislation is law passed by a legislative body.
Regulation is a set of rules or guidelines issued by an executive body such as government agencies or regulatory boards in compliance with the law.

• Parliaments pass laws. They are called legislation.
• Then government regulators have to enforce the law. They look at the law and work out the nitty-gritty details that need to be followed in order for the law to be followed.

Points for Consideration

Definition

1. (1) "children's recreation program" is a program operated by a children's recreation service provider listed in the Schedule to Regulation 797 of the Revised Regulations of Ontario, 1990 (Recreation Programs) made under the Ministry of Tourism and Reaction Act

1. (1) Added: (b) a program that meets the description set out in subsection 6 (4) of the Act

29-Aug-16

6 (4) of the Act: 1. The purpose of the program is to provide care 2. The program includes activities that promote recreational, artistic, musical or athletic skills or provides religious, cultural, or linguistic instruction 3. The program is not operated in a person’s home 4. The child care is provided for children six years old and older

Impact on: children’s recreation programs

New Definition

Not Applicable

1. (1) "family support program" has the same meaning as in Ontario Regulation 137/15 (General) made under the Act

29-Aug-16

Any of the following programs that receive funding from the Ministry of Education: 1. Child Care Resource Centres 2. Better Beginnings, Better Futures 3. Ontario Early Years Centres 4. Parenting and Family Literacy Centres

Impact on: family support programs

Definition

1. (1) "operating cost" means the gross expenditure, including administrative costs, reasonable and necessary for providing child care at a child care centre, home child care or a children's recreation program described in paragraph 8 of subsection 6 (1), less income other than revenue from fees

1. (1) "Operating cost" means the gross expenditure and includes: (a) administrative costs, (b) expenditures reasonable and necessary for providing child care at a child care centre, home child care or a children's recreation program described in paragraph 8 of subsection 6 (1), less income other than revenue from fees, and (c) expenditures reasonable and necessary for providing a family support program described in paragraph 9 of subsection 6 (1)

29-Aug-16

Costs for operation, paragraph 9 of subsection 6 (1) refers to: the establishment and provision of a family support program

Impact on: licensed child care centres, home child care and recreation programs

Subsection 54 (1) of the Act: The Minister may, (a) establish, administer, operate, and fund child care and early years programs and services, (b) provide financial assistance to individuals who are charged fees in respect of licensed child care, authorized recreational and skill building programs or extended day programs, in accordance with the regulations, and (c) fund and provide financial assistance for other programs or services prescribed by the regulations that provide or support temporary care/supervision of children.
