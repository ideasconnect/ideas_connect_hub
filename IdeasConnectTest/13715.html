Help Guide

Difference between Act and Regulations:
An Act is a legal document providing a general or broad description of legal policies.

Regulations are guidelines associated with an Act that guide how provisions of an Act are applied and should be followed.

Difference between Legislation and Regulation:
Legislation is law passed by a legislative body.
Regulation is a set of rules or guidelines issued by an executive body such as government agencies or regulatory boards in compliance with the law.

• Parliaments pass laws. They are called legislation.
• Then government regulators have to enforce the law. They look at the law and work out the nitty-gritty details that need to be followed in order for the law to be followed.

Licences issued under the Day Nurseries Act

89. (1) A licence or provisional licence issued under the Day Nurseries Act that was in force immediately before the day the Day Nurseries Act was repealed is continued as a licence or provisional licence, as the case may be, under the Child Care and Early Years Act, 2014. (2) The licence or provisional licence continued under subsection (1) continues to be subject to any terms, conditions or expiry date that applied to it under the Day Nurseries Act.

31-Aug-15

Licenses made under the Day Nurseries Act are still in force under the Child Care and Early Years Act, 2014

90. Approvals by a director made under the Day Nurseries Act are still in force under the Child Care and Early Years Act, 2014
